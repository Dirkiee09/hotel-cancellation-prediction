# AI Assistance Declaration

In accordance with Mapúa University's academic integrity policies and
modern best practice for transparent reporting of AI tool use in
research, the author declares the following AI tool usage during the
preparation of this thesis project.

## Tools Used

**Anthropic Claude (Claude Code, Claude Sonnet 4.x and Claude Opus 4.x
model families).** Used as a coding assistant and writing collaborator
across multiple sessions between 2025-09 and 2026-05. Accessed through
the official Claude Code command-line interface.

**Google Gemini (Gemini Pro 2.x family).** Used in a limited capacity
for external second-opinion code review, dependency-vulnerability
auditing, and presentation-coaching feedback during the final
defense-preparation phase (2026-05).

## What AI Assistance Was Used For

The author was the sole human author, designer, and operator of the
project. AI assistance was used for the following bounded purposes:

1. **Code generation under direction.** The author specified the
   architecture, requirements, methodology, and acceptance criteria;
   AI assistance accelerated the boilerplate (pandas pipelines,
   sklearn Pipeline construction, FastAPI endpoint scaffolds, pytest
   fixtures, matplotlib figure generators).
2. **Code review and debugging.** AI assistance was used to identify
   bugs, suggest fixes, and audit code for security and methodological
   issues (e.g., the falsy-zero bug in `resolve_thresholds`, the
   leakage-column drop ordering in the training pipeline).
3. **Documentation drafting.** The structure of `CLAUDE.md`,
   `README.md`, the notebook narrative cells, and chapter outlines
   were authored with AI assistance and then reviewed, edited, and
   approved by the author.
4. **Defense preparation.** The defense script, slide content guide,
   and practice Q&A file (under `docs/defense/`) were drafted with
   AI assistance, then verified against the actual artifacts before
   freeze.

## What AI Assistance Was NOT Used For

To preserve the integrity of the empirical claims, AI assistance was
explicitly **not** used for any of the following:

1. **Data collection or fabrication.** All datasets used in this
   thesis are sourced from documented, citable origins (the
   public Antonio et al. 2019 Portugal benchmark; the real
   Punta Villa Resort PMS export for the Philippine sub-study).
   No synthetic data was generated by AI.
2. **Results manipulation.** Every headline number reported in
   Chapters IV and V (e.g., PR-AUC 0.760, €2,937,754 cost-sensitive
   recovery, 97.5 % theoretical-maximum recovery, the −16.2 pp
   concept-drift gap) was computed by the trained model on the
   chronological test set and is reproducible from
   `reports/test_predictions_for_powerbi.csv` and
   `reports/metrics.json` via the verified pipeline.
3. **Hypothesis evaluation.** The five hypotheses (H1–H5) and their
   verdicts in Chapter IV §4.5.3 are the author's own pre-registered
   predictions, evaluated against the empirical SHAP rankings and
   paired-bootstrap p-values in
   `reports/benchmarks/14_paired_significance_vs_champion.csv`.
4. **Statistical inference.** Bootstrap confidence intervals
   (2,000 resamples) and paired significance tests were computed
   by the project's own statistical pipeline (`src/eval/statistical.py`),
   not generated by AI.

## Verification of AI-Drafted Content

All AI-drafted prose and code was reviewed and edited by the author
before inclusion in the thesis. Three forensic audits during the
defense-preparation phase removed AI-introduced inaccuracies,
including:

- 11 fabricated function names that did not exist in the codebase
  (caught and corrected in commit `f7dad4b`).
- 10 fabricated empirical claims of the form "I tested X and got Y"
  with no corresponding experiment on disk (caught and corrected
  in commit `a177e38`).
- 4 incorrect or fabricated figure-table cross-references (e.g.,
  citing Notebook 05 section 5.6 for SHAP dependence when the
  actual location is section 5.4).

Every numerical claim in the final committed thesis has been
independently recomputed against the source artifacts. Every cited
code path resolves to a function that exists on disk. Every cited
paper exists in the literature and is correctly attributed.

# Notes on Citation Style

This reference list follows **APA 7 (American Psychological
Association, 7th edition)** style as specified in Mapúa University's
Undergraduate Thesis Manual. The structure is:

1. **Author(s)** — Last name, Initials (separated by commas;
   ampersand before the last name)
2. **(Year)** — Publication year in parentheses
3. **Title** — Sentence case for articles; italicised for journal
   titles and book titles
